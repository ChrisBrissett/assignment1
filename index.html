Personal Information

Welcome and thank you for taking the time to read my Profile. My name is Christopher Brissett although my friends call me ‘Polar’, do not ask. I am 31 and currently work as an Operations Manager for an Industrial Supplies company, I have been doing this for about 4 years and I really enjoy the challenges the role gives me daily. The challenges which come with integrating and updating our current IT infrastructure have pushed me to pursue a career in IT, as I have always been fascinated by the intricate details that go with developing and managing such systems.

However, it is not all work no play for me. I am an avid gamer and I like to spend countless hours playing Xbox or PC whenever I can spare the time. I have what many may call an ‘Obsession’ with Formula 1, it doesn’t matter what time of the morning the race is on, you will find me sitting there glued to the TV watching every lap without fail.

Apart from most of my time being taken up with my work and hobby’s, I also like to use some Dutch Courage and get up on stage in front of people at Open mic comedy nights and talk absolute garbage for 10mins, I love being able to tell a joke and hopefully get a laugh.

Interest in IT

I have had a profound interest in IT for as long as I can remember. If I were to pick a moment where my interest began it would be as a young boy when I played a game, my father had designed using Java. It was a simple 2D game which was challenging to complete. What really grabbed my interest was that something I could enjoy to the level I did could be designed by some ‘code’, I spent hours playing this game and have always wanted to be able to develop something similar for others to enjoy like I did.

I have decided to come to RMIT through OUA to get myself a Bachelor of Information Technology. The flexibility to be able to learn and complete this degree from home and around my other commitments was the major factor for choosing to Enroll in the degree. After looking into what I wanted to get out of a higher education in the field of IT, I chose RMIT as I believe it will give me the skills and education needed to progress into my desired Industry of work once I have completed my Degree. This would be working on game development with the use of C++ programming.

Whilst now I do not have the greatest of knowledge around IT systems and technologies, I am of the strong belief at RMIT I will be able to acquire these skills and Education. This being in direct correlation to being able to develop, install & maintain computer systems and software’s. In addition to that also working through to gain a more detailed education in IT systems and Internet Design.
